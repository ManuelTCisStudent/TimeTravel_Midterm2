Movies Beyond Time and Against the Clock:

The Art of Time Travel Movies

The Art of Time Travel

The effectiveness of such a tale

Through cinematic media, Science Fiction has served as the overall basis for many creative and unique tales far from the typical tales within actual physics and logistics. Through Science Fiction, multiple sub-genre came alive on the screen, and my favorite subject that came from the realm of sci-fi is none other than Time Travel movies. As the name implies, time travel deal with the overall concept of what would happen if someone could go back to the past and change sometime, thusly changing their past, present, and future usually causing more harm than good. As such, my love for the media came from the many differing story telling and differing styles of movies allowed leeway by employing time travel. Of course, Time Travel is a risky subject as many fall under causing hazardous amount of plot holes to be developed within the story. However, even with these complexity, only the best can actually depict an complex, funny, or dramatic movie through imposing the complexity of time travel as a plot element. I'll be discussing a few of my favorite down below.

Back to the Future

A time travel classic among cinematic history

Back to the Future serves as perhaps one of the biggest franchises to employ the core theming around time travel within it's main plot. Rather than be a movie taking part into showing pure misery and the nightmare fuel effects of brought upon by messing with the past, Back to the Future (And its later two sequels) manages to showcase the danger brought in by time travel in a more friendly vibe, albeit still with serious consequences to keep the audience on their toes. Through this movie in particular came a more varied explosion of attempts to explore time travel from different studios, however nothing can beat a classic.

The Butterfly Effect

Time Travel meets misery

The Butterfly effect serves as a grim outlook into what would happen if good intention to fix the past just leads into more misery. This cult classic film serves to bring out the worst possible fate of those involved with time travel and serves as a cautionary tale against the concept of changing the past. To that end, The Butterfly Effect is a film that does a fine job at presenting its story and plot elements, while enticing the audince into the false belief that salvation can be find if one simply changes the negetive past.

Donnie Darko

Perhaps the most complex story of them all

Noteworthy as perhaps one of the most confusing and thought provoking tale concerning about time travel, Donnie Darko takes a more philosophical outlook onto our own existence through time travel, while forcing us to confront what truely makes you as a person. While many have written thesis videos/essays on the core story and implications of Donnie Darko, the overall nature of the film leaves many wondering about the complexity behind an concept such as time travel.
